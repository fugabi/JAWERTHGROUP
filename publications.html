Protein condensates as aging Maxwell fluids.

Science. (2020)

L. M. Jawerth, E. Fischer-Friedrich, Saha Suropriya, J. Wang, T. Franzmann, X. Zhang, J. Sachweh, M. Ruer, M. Ijavi, S. Saha, J. Mahamid, A. Hyman, F. Jülicher

In this paper, we carefully quantify the material properties of protein droplets over time. It is one of the first quantitative studies on these new materials. We find that dynamics of the droplets slow over time similar to aging observed in glasses. This is an important discovery and fundamentally alters how we view protein condensation. Link here

A Liquid-to-Solid Phase Transition of the ALS Protein FUS Accelerated by Disease Mutation

Cell. 162 (2015)

A. Patel*, H. O. Lee*, L. M. Jawerth, S. Maharana, M. Jahnel, M. Y. Hein, S. Stoynov, J. Mahamid, S. Saha, T. M. Franzmann, A. Pozniakovski, I. Poser, N. Maghelli, L. A. Royer, M. Weigert, E. W. Myers, S. Grill, D. Drechsel, A. A. Hyman, S. Alberti

In this paper, we show that the neurodegenerative disease protein FUS has liquid-like properties in cells. Moreover, we find that this droplet form can be recapitulated in vitro and over time the proteins convert into fibers. This conversion to fibers is accelerated for proteins associated with disease. Salt-dependent rheology and surface tension of protein condensates using optical traps

Physical review letters. 121, 258101 (2018)

L. M. Jawerth, M. Ijavi, M. Ruer, S. Saha, M. Jahnel, A. A. Hyman, F. Jülicher, E. Fischer-Friedrich

Here, we developed one of the first techniques to measure mechanical properties of protein condensates. I adapted common bulk rheology approaches for use with optical tweezers to actively measure time-resolved complex moduli and surface tension on a micron scale. Link here

Fibrin fibers have extraordinary extensibility and elasticity

Science. 313, 634–634 (2006)

4. W. Liu*, L. M. Jawerth*, E. Sparks, Mr. Falvo, R. Hantgan, R. Superfine, S. Lord, M. Guthold

In this paper, I built a new tool that incorporated an atomic force microscope and fluorescence microscope to measure the properties of individual fibrin fibers. We found that these are amongst the most extensible of all biopolymers. Link here
